T Wing takes power at Deli Aisle: what we know, who's upset, and what happens next

By Voss Harrity | Fort Worth, Texas | Updated 1 hour ago

The succession had been contentious since the departure of the previous owner, with at least three factions emerging within the Deli Aisle membership over the question of who should assume operational control. T Wing, widely regarded as a compromise candidate, had not publicly sought the role, according to two people familiar with the internal deliberations who spoke on the condition of anonymity because they were not authorized to share deli-related intelligence.

Freedough, who many had considered the frontrunner, did not immediately respond to the appointment. A source close to Freedough described the confirmation as "a gut punch delivered at 2 in the morning by someone who then immediately went offline," and said that conversations about next steps were "ongoing." Gustau, for his part, defended the selection in a brief statement posted to the #mod-announcements channel Thursday morning. "T Wing brings the structure this community needs," the statement read. "We are moving forward." The statement was followed by a single period and then nothing.

T Wing's first act as Head of Operations was to reorganize the server's channel architecture, merging #off-topic and #general into a single channel he renamed #the-floor. Several members expressed confusion. One asked whether their message history had been deleted. T Wing replied with a thumbs-up emoji and has not elaborated since.

The appointment has also renewed scrutiny of T Wing's previously stated positions on Wordle compliance. In a post from several months ago — now screenshotted and circulating widely — T Wing wrote that daily Wordle completion should be "strongly encouraged but ultimately personal," a stance that puts him in direct philosophical conflict with Gustau's mandatory completion policy. When asked about this discrepancy at an informal voice-chat session Thursday evening, T Wing said he was "still reviewing the literature" and that he would have more to say "when the time is right." The session ended shortly thereafter when someone's microphone picked up what appeared to be a running dishwasher.

Reaction across the broader community has been mixed. Several members expressed cautious optimism, citing T Wing's organizational track record and his reputation for responding to DMs within a reasonable timeframe. Others remain skeptical. "I don't know this person," wrote one long-standing member in a now-deleted post. "I have never known this person. I need someone to explain to me who this person is and why they are now in charge of things." As of Thursday night, T Wing had not yet posted his Wordle score.
