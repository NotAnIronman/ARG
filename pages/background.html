The Deli Aisle Network: a timeline

By Voss Harrity | Fort Worth, Texas | Updated 2 hours ago

2018 — Founding. August establishes the Deli Aisle Network as a private Discord server. Initial membership is small — fewer than 30 people. The server's stated purpose: coordinating daily Wordle completions and community discussion. No formal administrative structure exists at this time.

Early 2019 — Growth and formalization. Membership expands significantly. August introduces a tiered administrative structure, including moderator roles. Financial records later submitted in court indicate the organization begins generating revenue from membership subscriptions around this period. The source of an additional and significantly larger revenue stream is not established until much later.

April 2019 — First federal contact. According to a footnote on page 7 of the indictment later filed against August, the U.S. Attorney's office for the Southern District of New York — then under different leadership — opens "preliminary inquiries" into the Deli Aisle Network. The nature of those inquiries is not described in the public filing. No charges result. The inquiry is closed in November 2020.

November 2020 — Inquiry closed. The preliminary inquiry is closed without charge. No public explanation is given. August continues operating the server. A former colleague describes this period as one in which August became "more careful" about how the organization's finances were structured.

2021 — Leadership changes, new attention. Ricardo Saintiagoon is appointed U.S. Attorney for the Southern District of New York. Within four months of his appointment, a new inquiry into the Deli Aisle Network is opened — this time by Saintiagoon's own office. The speed of this reopening is noted in a later filing by August's defense as "circumstantially significant." The court does not address this characterization in its ruling.

Mid-2022 — Administrative restructuring. Internal records later obtained by Deli Aisle News show that August, in response to the renewed federal attention, restructures the server's administrative accounts. Several long-standing admin credentials are retired. New accounts are created. One account — later identified in server logs as g_admin_01 — is created during this period and assigned elevated permissions. The individual behind this account is not publicly identified in any court filing.

March 2023 — Indictment filed. August is formally indicted on charges of digital compliance evasion and conspiracy. The conspiracy count names an unnamed co-conspirator referred to only as "Individual A." August's legal team argues at arraignment that the conspiracy charge is unsupported by the evidence presented. The judge allows it to proceed. Individual A is never publicly identified.

August 2023 — August found injured in cell. August is discovered semiconscious in his cell at MCC Houston with marks on his neck. He is placed on suicide watch. The incident is reported internally and, eventually, to press. No formal investigation is opened into the circumstances.

September 2023 — Suicide watch discontinued. August is removed from suicide watch approximately two weeks after the cell incident. The decision is made by facility staff in consultation with a contracted mental health provider whose identity has not been made public. A formal request by August to be transferred to a different housing unit is filed and denied within 48 hours, with no stated reason.

October 1, 2023 — Death. August is found unresponsive in his cell at approximately 6:30 a.m. He is transported to a hospital and pronounced dead. The DOJ issues a statement describing the death as an apparent suicide. Two independent forensic consultants retained by August's legal team dispute this finding. Their full report has not been made public.

October 1, 2023, 11:58 p.m. Server access logs later obtained by cybersecurity researchers show an authenticated session beginning on the Deli Aisle Network's server at 11:58 p.m. — approximately 17.5 hours after August's death is announced. The session lasts 23 minutes. Administrative files, member records, and internal correspondence are extracted. The IP address associated with the session has not been publicly identified. Researchers describe it as "routed through a relay" but "traceable with the right access."

October 2023 — DOJ closes case. Case 7731-A is closed by the DOJ without further findings. Saintiagoon publicly states his own investigation is "ongoing." Bartholemew states the matter is "resolved." Neither explains the contradiction. O'mallaghathy issues a statement saying "someone out there knows what happened." No further official action is announced.
