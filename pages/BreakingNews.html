Breaking News

August, former owner of the Deli Aisle, found dead in federal custody.

Attorney General Nathan Barth calls the situation “deeply troubling” and promises a “full and transparent review.”
By Rylet Crash | Updated moments ago
August, 29, the former owner and operator of the popular Deli Aisle community server, was found unresponsive early Wednesday morning inside his federal holding cell in downtown Houston. Officials say he was not under any special monitoring at the time. His death comes just days after new documents were unsealed in his ongoing Wordle evasion case.

“This raises more questions than answers,” says longtime friend Enrique Holst

By Rylet Crash | Houston, Texas | Updated moments ago

Attorney General Nathan Barth said he was “deeply troubled” to learn of August’s death while in federal custody. He confirmed that he has contacted the Justice Department’s inspector general, who will “conduct a thorough review of all procedures and decisions made in the hours leading up to this incident.”

August’s death comes less than 48 hours after a set of previously sealed court documents were released, outlining new details about his alleged failure to complete the daily Wordle puzzle on April 16th. The documents also referenced “irregularities” in the Deli Aisle’s financial records, though officials declined to elaborate.

According to sources familiar with the case, August had recently requested access to archived transaction logs from the Deli Aisle’s internal systems. It is unclear why he sought the records, or whether they are connected to his sudden resignation on April 17th.

Enrique Holst, a close friend of August for more than a decade, expressed frustration at the lack of clarity. “He told me he was working on something important,” Holst said. “He didn’t say what. Now he’s gone, and we’re supposed to believe this was just… random?”

Federal officials say there is “no immediate indication of foul play,” though the FBI has opened an inquiry “out of an abundance of caution.” The Bureau does not typically investigate deaths in federal holding facilities unless circumstances are unusual.

Meanwhile, the Deli Aisle News website experienced a series of coordinated hacking attempts early Wednesday morning, around the same time August was discovered. The timing has fueled speculation online, though officials have not confirmed any connection.

Acting owner Gustau, who assumed leadership of the Deli Aisle on April 17th, has faced growing criticism for his strict enforcement of Wordle completion policies and his refusal to release August’s activity logs. Protesters outside the Deli Aisle storefront held signs reading “Show Us the Files” and “What Are You Hiding?”

Former coworker Patricio Olivarez, who worked closely with August, said the situation “doesn’t add up.” When asked to elaborate, he declined, saying only: “Someone needs to look at the numbers. All of them.”
