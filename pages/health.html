“He wasn’t depressed. He was scared.” A former staff member speaks.

By Prynne Delacourt | Fort Worth, Texas | Updated 7 hours ago

The former staff member, who left the facility several months before August’s death, described the housing unit where he was held as “a place that made everyone tense.” They cited ongoing maintenance issues, inconsistent staffing, and what they called “a general sense that things weren’t being handled the way they used to be.”

According to the source, August appeared “visibly anxious” in the weeks before his death. They emphasized that he did not seem depressed or withdrawn, but rather “alert in a way that suggested he was worried about something specific.”

The transfer request August submitted roughly ten days before his death is what the source found most unusual. “Those requests take time,” they said. “A week, sometimes two. His came back in two days. I’ve never seen that happen unless someone higher up got involved.”

The denial form, they said, contained no explanation — despite a designated field requiring one. “That box is supposed to be filled out. It wasn’t. That’s not normal procedure.”

When asked about the denial, a facility spokesperson said only that “all requests are processed according to policy.” They did not address the missing explanation or the unusually fast turnaround.

What August was afraid of

Mental health advocates who reviewed the account said the description aligns more closely with someone experiencing situational fear than with someone in a depressive crisis. “It sounds like he believed he was unsafe in that specific environment,” one advocate said.

The source recalled August asking repeatedly about the status of his request. “He wasn’t angry,” they said. “Just persistent. Like he knew he needed to be somewhere else.”

The advocates cautioned that secondhand accounts have limitations, but agreed that the unanswered question — why the request was denied so quickly, with no explanation — “deserves clarity.”

Unanswered questions

Publicly available records show no indication that August was evaluated for a housing change after the denial. There is also no record of a follow-up assessment, despite his repeated inquiries.

The former staff member said they believed August “knew something was wrong” but did not know whether he ever shared those concerns with anyone outside the facility.
