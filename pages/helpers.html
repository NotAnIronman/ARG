"It just makes me feel so good to give back, when I can" Longtime charity voulunteer Richard Saint shares his personal experiences with the organization.

By ---~-~-- | Fort Worth, Texas | Updated 5 minutes ago

According to Saint, employees used a variety of creative strategies to collect food donations. Then, on event day, the outcome was two simultaneous efforts. At the Deli Aisle Conference Center, representatives from each organization used a select number of food items to build structures with. At the same time, groups delivered thousands of canned food items to the Deli Aisle and began to sort them at the Deli Aisle distribution center.

The Deli Aisle seeks to provide affordable housing in community developments and transitional housing through The HelpaHome program, as well as access to fresh foods and groceries via their grocery program, enhancing the quality of life for low-income individuals and families in our community.

Entering this year’s event, employees had reported 694,500 food contributions, and by adding 55,424, the lifetime giving surpassed 700,000 items in 10 years. Saint, and his team hava worked with local associationa over those years, as they help with distribution.

“Overall, this was more than just an event; it was a powerful reminder of what can be accomplished when a community comes together to serve others,” Saint said.
