What we know about the key individuals in the August case — and the questions that remain about each of them.

As the investigation continues to raise more questions than answers, Deli Aisle News profiles the individuals at the center of the case. Some have spoken publicly. Some have not. None have been charged with any wrongdoing.

The individuals: what the record shows, what it doesn't, and what each of them has said

By Voss Harrity | Updated 35 minutes ago

None of the individuals profiled below have been charged with any crime in connection with August's death. This article reflects only what is documented in public records, court filings, and on-record statements.

Gustau — Current acting administrator, Deli Aisle Network

Gustau assumed operational control of the Deli Aisle Network following August's arrest and has remained in a leadership role since. Server logs show that an admin account designated g_admin_01 — believed to be associated with Gustau — was used in the server extraction of October 1st. Gustau posted a thumbs-up emoji on the server at 11:55 p.m. that night, three minutes before the extraction session began, and then went offline. He has not responded to any press inquiries in eleven days.

His defenders note that g_admin_01 credentials could have been cloned or stolen without his knowledge, and that his last known post is consistent with normal activity rather than premeditation. O'mallaghathy has said publicly that he does not believe Gustau acted alone — notably stopping short of saying Gustau acted at all.

Nathan Bartholemew — Attorney General

Bartholemew has made two contradictory public statements about the status of the August investigation. His original, pre-legal-review quote — obtained by Deli Aisle News — was "we have been told to consider this closed." The published version read "the matter has been effectively resolved at the departmental level." The distinction matters: one implies external pressure, the other implies independent judgment.

Sources close to Bartholemew describe him as "a man in an impossible position" who is aware of details he has been instructed not to disclose. He has not initiated any independent action since the case was closed. He has not resigned. He has also not defended Saintiagoon publicly, which several observers have noted is conspicuous given that the two men are technically on the same side.

Patrick O'mallaghathy — Former colleague of August

O'mallaghathy has been the most publicly vocal figure in questioning the official account of August's death. He has made references to knowing more than he can say publicly, described receiving "advice" about what to disclose, and posted — then deleted — a statement directly implicating Saintiagoon's knowledge of the case prior to the formal indictment.

He has no known financial connection to the Deli Aisle Network and no documented relationship with any federal agency. His critics suggest he is using the situation for attention. His supporters point out that of all the people in this case, he is the only one who has consistently pushed for more information rather than less.

Usain Holt — Childhood friend of August

Holt told Deli Aisle News shortly after August's death that he "forgot about the streak" and that August "got what was coming to him" — a comment widely shared and widely condemned. He has since declined all further press contact. His relationship with August in the months preceding the arrest is not well documented publicly, though a source with knowledge of the organization described Holt as someone who had "a complicated history with August that goes back a long way."

No financial records or server logs connect Holt to any administrative role in the Deli Aisle Network. His comment, however callous, does not constitute evidence of involvement. Several people who knew both men have described Holt's remark as consistent with a longstanding personal grievance rather than anything more serious.

T Wing — Current Head of Operations, Deli Aisle Network

T Wing was appointed Head of Operations in a 2:14 a.m. pinned message by Gustau the night following the October 1st server extraction. The timing has not gone unnoticed. T Wing has acknowledged the server access log findings and said he is "taking them seriously." He restructured the server's channel architecture within hours of his appointment and has not made himself available for interview.

No direct link between T Wing and the server extraction, the financial discrepancy in document 44-B, or any federal contact has been established. His appointment in the immediate aftermath of the extraction is either deeply suspicious or a coincidence of timing. He has offered no statement that would help distinguish between those two possibilities.

Ricardo Saintiagoon — U.S. Attorney, Southern District of New York

Saintiagoon is the individual about whom the most documented questions exist. The public record shows: his office opened a new inquiry into the Deli Aisle Network within four months of his appointment — an inquiry the indictment's own footnote indicates was preceded by a prior inquiry that his office, under different leadership, had conducted and closed. At the late-night briefing, he stated that August's death does not change "what I know" — a personal formulation he did not clarify when pressed. His official account of when he first became aware of the organization contradicts a footnote in his own indictment filing.

A portion of document 44-B — the financial disclosure showing an unexplained $340,000 — bears a partially redacted signatory on page 9 with three visible letters. The server extraction on October 1st originated from a federal building in lower Manhattan, according to a researcher who has provided a routing confirmation to Deli Aisle News. Saintiagoon's office is located in lower Manhattan.

The sealed supplement to the indictment, according to three sources with knowledge of its contents, identifies Individual A as a federal official. Saintiagoon has never been asked, on the record, whether he is Individual A. This newspaper has submitted that question in writing. No response has been received.
