Anonymous tip received by Fort Worth PD in connection with August death case; authorities confirm contents "warrant attention."

The tip, submitted through an online form at 3:14 a.m. on October 1st, has been forwarded to federal investigators. No further details have been released.
By Lena Forsyth | Updated 1 hour ago
Fort Worth police confirmed Thursday that an anonymous tip related to the death of August, the former operator of the Deli Aisle Network, had been received through the department's online submission portal in the early hours of October 1st and subsequently forwarded to the FBI field office in Houston. A department spokesperson described the tip's contents as "specific enough to warrant attention" but declined to elaborate. The tip was submitted at 3:14 a.m. — a time that, sources noted, corresponds to a window during which the MCC Houston facility's overnight staffing records show an unexplained 22-minute gap.

3:14 a.m., one anonymous tip, and a 22-minute gap that nobody has explained

By Lena Forsyth | Fort Worth, Texas | Updated 1 hour ago

The timing of the tip has drawn particular scrutiny. MCC Houston facility logs, obtained by Deli Aisle News through a public records request, show a 22-minute period — beginning at 3:09 a.m. on October 1st — during which no staff check-ins were recorded on the floor where August was held. The facility's standard protocol requires check-ins every 15 minutes during overnight hours. The gap was not flagged internally. It appears in the log only as blank space between two routine entries.

The Fort Worth Police Department declined to say whether the contents of the tip referenced the log gap specifically. A spokesperson said only that the tip had been submitted by "an individual who appeared to have direct knowledge of events at the facility" and that its contents had been determined to be "credible enough to escalate." The FBI confirmed receipt and said the tip was being "incorporated into the existing review." It declined to say whether the review was still ongoing — contradicting what the DOJ said last week about the case being closed.

Several residents in the neighborhood surrounding the federal building where August was held told Deli Aisle News they had noticed "unusual activity" in the weeks before his death. One woman, who asked not to be named, said she had seen vehicles she didn't recognize parked outside the building on multiple consecutive nights. Another said a man approached her near the facility asking questions she described as "too specific to be random" about the building's layout and entry points. Both accounts are unverified. Neither Fort Worth PD nor the FBI would confirm or deny that they were aware of them.

Patrick O'mallaghathy, who has been vocal about inconsistencies in the official account, said Thursday that he was "not surprised" a tip had come in. "Someone out there knows what happened," he said. "And they've been sitting on it, waiting for the right moment. Maybe that moment is now." He paused. "Or maybe someone is trying to control what comes out and when. I genuinely don't know which."
