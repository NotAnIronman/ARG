Anonymous tip received by Fort Worth PD in connection with August case; authorities confirm contents “warrant attention.”

The tip, submitted through an online form at 3:14 a.m. on October 1st, has been forwarded to investigators. No further details have been released.
By Lena Forsyth | Updated 1 hour ago
Fort Worth police confirmed Thursday that an anonymous tip related to the death of August, former operator of the Deli Aisle Network, was received through the department’s online portal in the early hours of October 1st. A spokesperson described the tip as “specific enough to warrant attention.” The timestamp — 3:14 a.m. — corresponds to a 22‑minute gap in the facility’s overnight activity log.

3:14 a.m., one anonymous tip, and a 22‑minute gap nobody has explained

By Lena Forsyth | Fort Worth, Texas | Updated 1 hour ago

The timing of the tip has drawn particular scrutiny. Facility logs obtained by Deli Aisle News show a 22‑minute period — beginning at 3:09 a.m. on October 1st — during which no staff check‑ins were recorded on the floor where August was housed. Standard protocol requires check‑ins every 15 minutes. The gap appears only as blank space between two routine entries.

Fort Worth PD declined to say whether the tip referenced the gap directly. A spokesperson said only that the tip “appeared to come from someone with direct knowledge of the environment” and that its contents were “credible enough to escalate.”

Investigators confirmed receipt and said the tip was being “incorporated into the ongoing review.” They did not clarify whether the review is still active, despite earlier statements suggesting the case had been closed.

Several residents near the facility reported noticing unusual activity in the weeks before August’s death. One resident described unfamiliar vehicles parked outside the building on multiple nights. Another said a man approached her asking questions about the building’s layout that she described as “too specific to be casual.”

Both accounts remain unverified. Authorities declined to confirm whether they were aware of these reports.

Patricio Olivarez, a former coworker of August’s, said he was “not surprised” a tip had surfaced. “Someone out there knows what happened,” he said. “Maybe they finally decided to say something. Or maybe someone decided it was time for something to be said.”
