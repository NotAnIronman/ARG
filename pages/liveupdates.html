Live Updates

Overnight developments: case 7731-A, the briefing, and what happens next

Live blog by Deli Aisle News Desk | Last updated 3:22 a.m.
3:22 A.M.
IP address from server extraction traced to administrative complex in Manhattan — researcher confirms routing path
A cybersecurity researcher analyzing the Deli Aisle Network’s server logs contacted Deli Aisle News with a significant update: the IP address used in the October 1st extraction has been traced, through multiple relays, to a restricted-access administrative complex in lower Manhattan. The researcher declined to identify the specific office or floor, citing personal safety concerns. “I know exactly where it terminates,” they said. “But I’m not saying it publicly until I know who else already knows.” The routing confirmation document is referenced internally as /files/ip_routing_confirm.txt.
2:47 A.M.
Olivarez posts and deletes a statement; screenshot circulates
Former Deli Aisle coworker Patricio Olivarez posted a short message to his personal account at approximately 2:40 a.m. It read: “Some people knew long before the rest of us. Ask what changed in April.” The post was deleted within minutes. Screenshots are circulating. Olivarez has not responded to follow-up messages.
1:55 A.M.
Statement released: “ongoing review prevents further comment”
A brief three-sentence statement was released by a communications representative connected to the case. It acknowledges “public interest in the briefing” and says that “the ongoing review prevents further comment at this time.” It does not address the discrepancies raised during the briefing, the missing transcript minutes, or the identity of the individual referenced in sealed documents.
1:14 A.M.
Official briefing transcript released — 15 minutes shorter than recorded audio
The official transcript accounts for 19 minutes of content. The audio recording captured by a reporter in the room runs 34 minutes. Deli Aisle News has submitted a request for clarification regarding the missing 15 minutes. No response has been received.
12:38 A.M.
Communications office unreachable following briefing
Multiple attempts to reach the communications office associated with the case went unanswered. An automated message states the office is “closed until morning.” A secondary number listed on the public directory routes to an inactive voicemail box.
11:47 P.M.
“What I know” — phrasing from briefing draws immediate attention
During the briefing, a key official stated: “The death of the primary subject does not change what we know. It doesn’t change what I know.” Reporters noted the shift from “we” to “I.” A follow-up question requesting clarification was deflected. The official was not asked about earlier inquiries referenced in archived documents — those details emerged only after reporters reviewed the original filings.
11:30 P.M.
Unscheduled briefing begins — Deli Aisle News not on invite list
The briefing was announced with less than 90 minutes’ notice and held in a secondary room. Several outlets that have closely followed the case were not included on the initial invite list. A Deli Aisle News reporter arrived independently and was admitted. The briefing opened with a prepared statement.
