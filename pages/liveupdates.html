Overnight developments: case 7731-A, the briefing, and what happens next

Live blog by Deli Aisle News Desk | Last updated 3:22 a.m.
3:22 A.M.
IP address from server extraction traced to federal building in Manhattan — researcher confirms, declines to name specific office
An independent cybersecurity researcher who has been analyzing the Deli Aisle Network's server access logs contacted Deli Aisle News in the past hour with a significant update: the IP address used in the October 1st server extraction has been traced, through a relay chain, to a federal building in lower Manhattan. The researcher declined to name the specific office or agency, citing concern for their own safety. "I know which floor," they said. "I'm not going to say that right now." They said they had shared the routing confirmation with a lawyer and asked that it not be published in full until "certain people are aware." Deli Aisle News is withholding the specific building address pending further verification and legal review. The routing confirmation document is referenced in our internal file system as /files/ip_routing_confirm.txt.
2:47 A.M.
O'mallaghathy posts and then deletes a statement; screenshot is circulating
Patrick O'mallaghathy posted a brief statement to his personal account at approximately 2:40 a.m. It read, in full: "Saintiagoon has known since before the indictment. Ask him what he was doing in April 2019." The post was deleted within four minutes. Screenshots are circulating widely. O'mallaghathy has not responded to follow-up messages. His phone goes directly to voicemail.
1:55 A.M.
Saintiagoon's office releases brief statement: "ongoing investigation prevents further comment"
The statement, three sentences long, says Saintiagoon's office is "aware of media coverage surrounding the briefing" and that "the ongoing nature of the investigation prevents further public comment at this time." It does not address the discrepancy between his stated position and Bartholemew's, the question of Individual A, or the footnote on page 7 of the indictment. It is signed by a communications staffer, not by Saintiagoon himself.
1:14 A.M.
DOJ releases official briefing transcript — 15 minutes shorter than the recorded audio
The official transcript runs to four pages and accounts for approximately 19 minutes of content. The audio recording taken by a reporter in the room runs 34 minutes. Deli Aisle News has submitted a written request to the DOJ's press office asking what accounts for the discrepancy. No response has been received.
12:38 A.M.
Bartholemew's office does not respond to calls after briefing ends
Multiple attempts to reach the Attorney General's communications office following the briefing went unanswered. An automated message says the office is "closed until 8 a.m." A duty officer number listed on the DOJ website rings through to a voicemail that has not been set up.
11:47 P.M.
Saintiagoon says "what I know" — reporters in the room immediately note the phrasing
During the briefing, Saintiagoon says: "The death of the primary defendant does not change what we know. It doesn't change what I know." Three reporters present independently flagged the shift from institutional "we" to personal "I" in their notes. A follow-up question asking him to clarify was deflected. He was not asked about his office's preliminary inquiries from April 2019 during the briefing itself — that detail emerged afterward, from reporters reviewing the original indictment.
11:30 P.M.
Unscheduled briefing begins — Deli Aisle News was not on the invite list
The briefing was called with less than 90 minutes' notice and held in a secondary room at the federal building. Several outlets that have been most actively covering the August case were not on the initial press list. A Deli Aisle News reporter arrived independently and was admitted. Bartholemew begins by reading a prepared statement.
