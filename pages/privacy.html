Privacy Policy

Effective date: August 31, 2026 — Version 2.0

1. Information we collect

When you visit Deli Aisle News, we may collect standard browsing data including IP addresses, browser type, pages visited, and time spent on site. If you subscribe to our newsletter or create an account, we also collect your email address and any profile information you provide.

Note: Access attempts flagged as “automated” or “nonstandard” may be logged separately for security review.

2. How we use your information

We use collected data to operate our website, improve our services, send newsletters to subscribers, and analyze site traffic. We do not sell personal data to third parties. We do not use your data for targeted advertising.

Certain aggregated data may be used internally for security audits.

3. Cookies

We use cookies for site analytics and to remember user preferences. You may disable cookies in your browser settings. Disabling cookies may affect site functionality.

A temporary session cookie is created when accessing the secure tip form. This cookie expires automatically.

4. Third-party services

We use third-party analytics services to understand site traffic. These services may collect data in accordance with their own privacy policies. We do not share personally identifying information with analytics providers.

Third-party logs do not include content from secure submissions.

5. Data retention

We retain subscriber data for as long as a subscription is active, plus 12 months. Browsing data is retained for no more than 90 days. Tip submissions are retained for a period determined by the investigations desk in accordance with source protection obligations.

Metadata associated with tip submissions may be retained longer for security review.

6. Your rights

You may request access to, correction of, or deletion of your personal data by contacting privacy@deliaislnews.com. We will respond to such requests within 30 days.

Requests related to secure submissions may require additional verification.

7. Disclosure to authorized federal partners [2]

In certain circumstances, Deli Aisle News may share user data — including tip submission metadata, IP logs, and subscriber records — with authorized federal partners where required by applicable law or pursuant to a valid legal instrument. Such disclosures will be made in accordance with our legal obligations and, where permissible, we will notify affected users prior to disclosure.

The term “authorized federal partners” refers to agencies or entities that have been granted lawful access to such information under applicable statute. A full list of such partners is not published here for operational reasons. [2]

8. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy periodically. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated effective date. Continued use of our site constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

Previous versions of this policy are available upon request.

[1] Analytics data is processed in accordance with applicable data protection law.

[2] For questions about this section, contact legal@deliaislnews.com.
