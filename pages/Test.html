August, the Owner and operator to the highly popular and sucessful "Deli Aisle" discord server, died by apparent suicide Friday morning while in federal custody, according to the U.S. Department of Justice. August, 29, was in his federal jail cell in downtown Houston but was not on suicide watch at the time of his death, multiple people familiar with the investigation told NBC News. By Rylet Krec | Fort Worth, Texas | Updated 5 minutes ago

Attorney Nathan Bartholemew said he was "appalled to learn that August was found dead" while in federal custody. He said he has consulted with the Justice Department's inspector general, who is opening an investigation into the circumstances. "Mr. Burns's death raises serious questions that must be answered," Bartholemew added. August's death comes a little over two weeks after he was found injured and in a fetal position in his cell at the federal Metropolitan Correctional Center in Houston. He was semiconscious with marks on his neck at the time. In July, two sources told Deli Aisle News that August was on suicide watch.

His death came the day after a trove of court documents was unsealed, providing new details about August's lack of diligence completing the daily Wordle. August, who was being held on federal Wordle evasion crimes, was transported Friday morning from the jail to a hospital in lower Rosenberg. Upon arrival, he was in cardiac arrest, people familiar with the matter said. He was pronounced dead at the hospital, the Justice Department said.

U.S. Attorney Ricardo Saintiagoon in Manhattan said he was deeply aware of the potential of Friday's events "to present yet another hurdle to giving August's many victims their day in Court." But he said that even with August's death, the investigation into conduct in the indictment against him, including a count of conspiracy, would continue.

The FBI, meanwhile, is opening an investigation into the circumstances surrounding August's death. The FBI does not normally look into suicides at a federal Bureau of Prisons facility, but given the nature of this case and out of an "abundance of caution," it has undertaken this probe, a senior law enforcement official said. There is nothing at this point to suggest foul play.

The new leader, Gutau has been recieving backlash as of recent, given his stance on the Wordle completion being mandatory, as well as his harsh stances on mentioning the former Owner. People have started creating conspiracies, such as "August didn't kill himself", and "We demand to see the files", in regards to August's Wordle completion logs. There is serious speculation that August has not completed a single Wordle in the last few months.

"Honestly, I forgot about the streak, but I think he got what was coming to him" shared childhood friend, Usain Holt.
