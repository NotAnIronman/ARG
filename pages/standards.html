Editorial Standards & Practices

Last reviewed: September 15, 2026 — Version 4.0

1. Accuracy

Deli Aisle News is committed to factual accuracy in all published material. Before publication, all factual claims must be verified by at least two independent sources or supported by primary documentation. Reporters are required to seek comment from all named parties before publication where reasonably possible.

2. Independence

Editorial decisions are made solely by the Editorial team and are not subject to influence from advertising partners, funders, or any external party. No coverage may be offered, promised, or withheld in exchange for financial consideration.

3. Source protection

Deli Aisle News will protect the identities of confidential sources. Reporters may not disclose source identities to any third party, including law enforcement, without the source's explicit consent and consultation with editorial leadership and legal counsel.

4. Corrections policy

Factual errors identified after publication will be corrected promptly. Corrections will be appended to the relevant article and noted in the daily corrections log. Minor errors will be corrected inline with a note. Significant errors will be addressed with a formal correction statement.

5. Retraction policy

Articles will be retracted only in cases of serious factual failure, fabrication, or where publication is found to cause direct harm that outweighs the public interest in the information. Retraction decisions are made by the Editor-in-Chief in consultation with legal counsel.

In cases where retraction is requested by a subject of coverage, a federal agency, or any party with a legal interest in the material, Deli Aisle News will evaluate the request on its merits and publish its reasoning for any decision made, whether or not the request is granted.

— Note: the above clause was under review as of v4.0. The revised policy will be published when finalized.

6. Conflicts of interest

Staff members must disclose any personal, financial, or professional relationship with individuals or organizations they cover. Reporters may not cover stories in which they have a direct conflict of interest without editorial approval and disclosure to readers.

7. Anonymous sources

Anonymous sources will be used only when the information is in the public interest and cannot be obtained through on-the-record means, and when the reporter and editor are satisfied that the source has direct knowledge of the facts they are describing. The reason for anonymity will be noted in the article where possible.

8. External pressure

Deli Aisle News does not alter, withhold, or suppress coverage in response to legal threats, government pressure, or requests from any party with a financial or political interest in the outcome. Any such requests received will themselves be reported as news where appropriate.

Questions about our editorial standards? Contact standards@deliaislnews.com. To report a potential standards violation by our staff, contact ombudsman@deliaislnews.com.
