Two senior officials issue conflicting statements on the August investigation within a single news cycle.

Analysts say the discrepancy is “either a communication failure or something more deliberate.” Neither office has explained the contradiction.
By Cass Hullery | Updated 2 hours ago
Senior Administrator Nathan Barth stated Thursday morning that the inquiry into August’s death was “functionally concluded,” describing the matter as “settled pending routine closure.” Six hours later, Regional Oversight Director Ricardo Saint issued a contradictory statement, calling the inquiry “active, ongoing, and nowhere near resolution.” Both statements were made on the record. Neither office has responded to requests to reconcile them.

“Functionally concluded” vs. “nowhere near resolution”: two officials, one case, zero explanation

By Cass Hullery | Capitol District | Updated 2 hours ago

Barth’s statement came during a brief press line outside the Administrative Annex Thursday morning. Asked whether the inquiry into August’s death remained open, he said:

“That matter is functionally concluded. We have reviewed what we need to review. The record is clear.”

He did not take follow-up questions.

Saint’s statement came six hours later, in response to a nearly identical question:

“The inquiry into the conduct described in the original filing is active and ongoing. The death of the primary subject does not resolve the outstanding issues.”

When asked how both statements could be true, Barth’s office said the two officials were “referring to different procedural tracks.” They did not specify which tracks. Saint’s office did not respond.

Three governance analysts contacted by Deli Aisle News described the contradiction as “unusual,” “structurally inconsistent,” and “the kind of thing that happens when someone receives unexpected instructions.”

The original filing against August included charges of digital compliance evasion and a single count of conspiracy. The conspiracy count implied the involvement of at least one additional party, referred to only as “Individual A.” Neither office has ever addressed questions about Individual A.

Deli Aisle News submitted written questions about the identity of Individual A in September. Neither office has responded.

Former colleague Patricio Olivarez, who has spoken publicly about inconsistencies in the official account, said Thursday’s conflicting statements were “exactly what people should be paying attention to.” He declined to elaborate, citing “guidance” he had received.

When asked who provided that guidance, Olivarez paused. “Someone who knows more than I do,” he said. “And who is nervous.”
