"Effectively resolved" vs. "far from concluded": two officials, one case, zero explanation

By Cass Hullery | Washington, D.C. | Updated 2 hours ago

Bartholemew's statement came during a brief gaggle outside the Eisenhower Building Thursday morning. Asked directly whether the federal investigation into the circumstances of August's death remained open, he said: "That matter has been effectively resolved at the departmental level. We have been thorough. We are satisfied with what the record shows." He did not take follow-up questions.

Saintiagoon's statement came six hours later, to a reporter from a separate outlet who had asked a nearly identical question. "The investigation into the conduct outlined in the original indictment — including the conspiracy count — is active and ongoing," Saintiagoon said. "The death of the primary defendant does not extinguish the legal questions the indictment raises. We are continuing our work."

When asked by Deli Aisle News to explain how both statements could be true simultaneously, a spokesperson for the Attorney General's office said the two men were "speaking to different aspects of a multifaceted legal situation." The spokesperson did not specify which aspects. Saintiagoon's office did not respond. Three legal analysts contacted by Deli Aisle News described the contradiction as "unusual," "concerning," and "the kind of thing that happens when someone gets a phone call they weren't expecting."

The original indictment against August, filed in March, included charges of digital compliance evasion and a single count of conspiracy. The conspiracy count, legal experts noted at the time, implied the involvement of at least one other party. That party has never been publicly identified. The indictment referred to them only as "Individual A." Neither Bartholemew nor Saintiagoon has ever been asked publicly about Individual A. Deli Aisle News submitted a written question on this subject to both offices in September. Neither has responded.

Former colleague Patrick O'mallaghathy, who has spoken publicly several times about inconsistencies in the official account, said Thursday's contradictory statements were "exactly what I've been trying to tell people." He declined to elaborate further, citing what he described as "advice I've been given about what to say publicly right now." When pressed on who gave him that advice, O'mallaghathy paused for a long moment. "Someone who knows more than I do," he said. "And who is nervous."
