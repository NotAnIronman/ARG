"I've known about this organization for some time": what Saintiagoon said at the briefing — and what didn't make the official transcript

By Cass Hullery | Fort Worth, Texas | Updated 11 minutes ago

The briefing opened with Bartholemew reading a prepared statement confirming that the DOJ considered case 7731-A "resolved at the departmental level." He took no questions. He then introduced Saintiagoon, who he described as being present "to speak to the prosecutorial dimension of the ongoing matter." Saintiagoon, who arrived 40 minutes into an event that had been running for 20, did not acknowledge his late arrival. He stepped to the podium, adjusted the microphone, and said: "I want to be clear that my office's investigation is continuing. The death of the primary defendant does not change what we know. It doesn't change what I know."

That last sentence — "what I know," in the first person singular rather than the institutional "we" — drew immediate attention from the reporters in the room. Saintiagoon did not elaborate. A follow-up question asking him to clarify what, specifically, he personally knew was deflected: "This isn't the venue for that conversation."

Later in the briefing, a reporter asked Saintiagoon directly when he had first become aware of the Deli Aisle Network. His answer — "through the standard referral process, when the case was assigned to my office" — was inconsistent with a detail buried in the original indictment filing: a footnote on page 7 indicating that Saintiagoon's office had "conducted preliminary inquiries into the organization" more than eight months before the formal indictment was filed. When this was raised, Saintiagoon said he "didn't have the filing in front of him" and moved on. Bartholemew, standing to his left, did not react.

O'mallaghathy, reached by phone after the briefing ended, had a single response when told what Saintiagoon had said. "Eight months," he repeated. "And nobody asked him what he was doing for those eight months?" He declined further comment and ended the call.

The official transcript of the briefing, released by the DOJ's press office at 1:14 a.m., runs to four pages. The audio recording of the same briefing, taken by a reporter present in the room, runs to 34 minutes. The transcript accounts for approximately 19 minutes of content. Deli Aisle News has requested clarification on what accounts for the remaining 15 minutes. No response has been received.
