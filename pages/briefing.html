"I've known about this organization for some time": what Saint said at the briefing — and what didn't make the official transcript

By Cass Hullery | Fort Worth, Texas | Updated 11 minutes ago

The briefing opened with Attorney General Nathan Barth reading a prepared statement confirming that the DOJ considered case 7731-A “administratively concluded.” He emphasized that “the department remains committed to transparency,” though he declined to take questions. Barth’s tone was calm and measured, and he repeatedly stressed that “all proper procedures were followed.”

He then introduced U.S. Attorney Ricardo Saint, who he described as being present “to address the prosecutorial dimension.” Saint arrived 40 minutes after Barth and did not acknowledge his late entrance. He stepped to the podium, adjusted the microphone, and said: “I want to be clear that my office’s investigation is continuing. The death of the primary defendant does not change what we know. It doesn’t change what I know.”

That last sentence — “what I know,” in the first person singular — immediately drew attention. Saint did not elaborate. When asked to clarify what he personally knew, he responded: “This isn’t the venue for that conversation.”

A reporter later asked Saint when he had first become aware of the Deli Aisle Network. His answer — “through the standard referral process, when the case was assigned to my office” — contradicted a detail in the original indictment: a footnote on page 7 indicating that his office had “conducted preliminary inquiries into the organization” more than eight months before the formal indictment was filed.

When pressed on the discrepancy, Saint said he “didn’t have the filing in front of him” and moved on. Barth, standing beside him, maintained a neutral expression and did not intervene.

Olivarez, reached by phone after the briefing, reacted strongly when told of the eight‑month reference. “Eight months,” he repeated. “And nobody asked him what he was doing for those eight months?” He declined further comment.

The official transcript of the briefing, released by the DOJ press office at 1:14 a.m., runs four pages. The audio recording taken by a reporter in the room runs 34 minutes. The transcript accounts for approximately 19 minutes of content. Deli Aisle News has requested clarification on the missing 15 minutes. No response has been received.

Notably, Barth’s prepared remarks lasted just over 90 seconds — unusually brief for an Attorney General addressing a high‑profile in‑custody death. His statement made no mention of the hacking attempts on the Deli Aisle News website earlier that same morning, nor of August’s recent request for archived transaction logs from the Deli Aisle’s internal systems.
